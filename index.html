In 2006, Laura Bush said that America was going to Afghanistan to fight "the brutal oppression of women". While we were busy fighting sexual oppression (among other things) in Afghanistan, we also tried to reduce sexual assaults in our own military, even if it didn't succeed at the start.

We won some major victories against the Taliban, and even established democracy in Afghanistan. Its women thanked us for helping them feel safe. Our own women were playing a greater role within the armed forces, and we tried again to reduce sexual assaults in our own military. But, it still didn't seem to work.

We struggled to keep the peace, and Afghanistan slowly descended into chaos. As assaults against Afghan women continued to rise, we kept pretending to stymie sexual assaults in our own military. But, clearly, we weren't doing enough for women, whether in Afghanistan or in our armed forces.

Can we help women elsewhere in the world if we don't pay attention to our own?
